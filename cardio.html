Cardio Zone Dose Calculator

Adjust work-session length and weekly frequency. The calculator assigns productive training time across Z2–Z5, while Z1 is shown separately as warm-up/recovery overhead.

Core idea: Zone 1 is preparation and recovery, not the main work dose. Productive adaptation time is distributed across Z2–Z5, with more high-output work when total time is low and more Zone 2 when time is high.

1. Choose training status

How to choose your level

Use your weakest limiter. If your volume, hard-effort tolerance, or recovery fits a lower category, choose the lower level.

Beginner / Deconditioned <6 weeks consistent training · <90 min/week · hard efforts wreck you for 48h+
Intermediate 6–16 weeks consistent · 90–180 min/week · can repeat 1–3 min hard efforts
Trained / Advanced 4+ months structured · 180–360 min/week · can repeat 3–4 min Z4 blocks
Elite / Competitive Long-term structured training · 360+ min/week · high intensity does not disrupt the week

2. Choose adaptation focus

What do the focus options mean?

Focus shifts emphasis, not exclusivity. The unchosen systems still receive maintenance work so one part of fitness does not degrade.

All 3 balanced Best default. Builds aerobic base, heart/stroke-volume stimulus, and VO2max together.
MITO focus Mitochondrial/aerobic base: better endurance, fat oxidation, and ability to handle more total work. Mostly Z2/Z3, with Z4/Z5 maintained.
CARDIAC focus Heart/stroke-volume stimulus: sustained high cardiac output that supports lower resting HR and stronger cardiovascular output. Mostly Z3/Z4.
VO2max focus Aerobic ceiling: your ability to use oxygen at high intensity. Mostly Z4 with some Z5, while Z2/Z3 stay at maintenance.
